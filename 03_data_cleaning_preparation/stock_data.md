# Financial Market Symbols Overview

This document provides a breakdown of common ticker symbols used in global financial markets, including commodities, stock indices, and currency indices.

---

## 1. Asset Descriptions

### **XAUUSD** (Gold / US Dollar)
- **Type**: Commodity / Currency Pair
- **Description**: Represents the price of **one troy ounce of Gold** in US Dollars. 
- **Sentiment**: Often viewed as a "safe-haven" asset; prices typically rise during periods of high inflation or geopolitical instability.

### **US500** (S&P 500 Index)
- **Type**: Stock Market Index
- **Description**: Tracks the performance of the **500 largest companies** listed on stock exchanges in the United States.
- **Sentiment**: The primary benchmark for the health of the US equity market.

### **UKOIL** (Brent Crude Oil)
- **Type**: Commodity
- **Description**: The global benchmark for **Brent Crude Oil**. It tracks oil produced in the North Sea.
- **Sentiment**: Highly sensitive to global supply/demand and Middle Eastern geopolitics.

### **DXY** (US Dollar Index)
- **Type**: Currency Index
- **Description**: Measures the value of the **US Dollar** against a basket of six major world currencies (Euro, Yen, Pound, Canadian Dollar, Swedish Krona, and Swiss Franc).
- **Sentiment**: A high DXY indicates a strong US Dollar relative to other global currencies.

### **GER40** (DAX 40)
- **Type**: Stock Market Index
- **Description**: Tracks the **40 largest German companies** trading on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange.
- **Sentiment**: The main indicator for the strength of the German (and by extension, European) economy.
